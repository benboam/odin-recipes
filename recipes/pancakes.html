Pancakes

Description

Pancakes are quick griddle-cakes made from a simple batter—flour, milk, egg, a little sugar, and baking powder—poured onto a hot, lightly greased pan. As they cook, bubbles rise and pop, the edges set, and a flip gives you a golden, tender centre with a lightly crisp surface. American-style pancakes are thick and fluffy; crêpes are thin and lacy; Scotch pancakes (drop scones) sit somewhere in between. All start the same way: whisk, rest briefly, cook, flip, stack.

They’re a blank canvas for toppings and add-ins: butter and maple syrup, berries, yoghurt, lemon and sugar, honey, or chocolate chips and banana. You can go savoury too—think sweetcorn and spring onion with a dollop of sour cream, or smoked salmon and crème fraîche on crêpes. Swap in gluten-free flour, dairy-free milk, or a flax “egg” if needed, and you’ve got breakfast (or dessert) sorted.

Ingredients

  • 190 g plain/all-purpose flour (1½ cups)
  • 2 tbsp sugar (caster or granulated)
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp fine salt
  • 300 ml milk (1¼ cups) — or 300 ml buttermilk + ½ tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tbsp melted butter (or neutral oil)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract (optional)
  • Butter or oil for the pan
  • Optional add-ins: 100 g blueberries (¾ cup), 75 g chocolate chips (½ cup), lemon zest, pinch of cinnamon

Steps

  1. Whisk flour, sugar, baking powder, and a pinch of salt in a bowl.
  2. In another bowl, whisk milk, egg, and melted butter (or oil) until smooth.
  3. Pour wet into dry; stir gently just until combined (a few lumps are fine).
  4. Rest the batter 5–10 minutes to hydrate and puff.
  5. Heat a nonstick pan over medium and lightly grease.
  6. Ladle batter into the pan, leaving space between pancakes.
  7. Cook until bubbles form on top and edges look set, 2–3 minutes.
  8. Flip and cook 1–2 minutes more until golden and cooked through.
  9. Keep warm on a low oven tray while you finish the batch.
  10. Serve with butter, syrup, and fruit—or whatever toppings you love.
