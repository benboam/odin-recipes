Avocado Toast

Description

Avocado toast is the ultra-simple, ultra-satisfying combo of creamy mashed avocado piled onto hot, crunchy toast. A squeeze of lemon or lime, a pinch of salt, and a few cracks of black pepper wake everything up; chilli flakes or hot sauce add a gentle kick. It’s fast (five minutes, tops), filling without feeling heavy, and built for tweaking—keep it chunky or smooth, drizzle with good olive oil, and you’re done.

From there, you can dress it up or keep it minimal. Add a poached or fried egg for extra protein, crumble on feta, scatter cherry tomatoes or radishes for freshness, or dust with everything seasoning or za’atar. Swap sourdough for wholegrain, rye, or whatever you have. It’s breakfast, lunch, or snack food that feels a little special with almost no effort.

Ingredients

  • 2 slices good bread (sourdough or wholegrain)
  • 1 ripe avocado
  • 1–2 tsp lemon or lime juice
  • pinch of flaky salt
  • black pepper
  • chilli flakes or a dash of hot sauce (optional)
  • 1–2 tsp extra-virgin olive oil (optional)

Steps

  1. Toast the bread slices until golden and crisp.
  2. Halve the avocado, remove the pit, and scoop the flesh into a bowl.
  3. Add the lemon or lime juice, a pinch of flaky salt, black pepper, and chilli flakes or hot sauce (if using).
  4. Mash to your preferred texture (chunky or smooth) and taste-adjust seasoning.
  5. Spread the avocado evenly over the hot toast.
  6. Drizzle with extra-virgin olive oil (if using).
  7. Optional: add toppings like a poached/fried egg, feta, tomatoes, radish, onions, fresh herbs, or everything seasoning.
  8. Serve immediately.
